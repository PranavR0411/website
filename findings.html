Chapter 07 — Conclusions · thesis ch. 9

What Was Learned

Three contributions, honestly graded. What the framework established, where it stops short, and the unusually concrete roadmap from this thesis to the next generation of sky surveys.

The three contributions, scored

Contribution I

VILA — the IGM absorption, per galaxy

Replacing the one-curve-fits-all IGM correction with 1,000 Monte Carlo sightlines per galaxy, fused by a Gaussian copula, cut the per-filter outlier fraction from 17.0% (mean-curve correction) and 11.6% (no correction at all) to 2.26% — reductions of 87% and 80%. The most IGM-sensitive filters tell it best: HSC g fell from 14.3% to 0.19%, HSC r from 24.5% to 0.03%, HSC i from a disastrous 98.7% to 0.32%. The headline insight along the way: the standard mean correction doesn't just underperform — it actively overcorrects, because the fitted coefficients have already silently absorbed part of the IGM. Averaging the fog is worse than ignoring it.

Contribution II

The population model — spectrum-space, mapped

A twice-modified Extreme Deconvolution — piecewise-linear redshift-dependent mean across seven bins, Student-t robustness against galaxies' heavy tails — converged stably in under 50 iterations, its parameters settling by six orders of magnitude. The generative test: model and data magnitude distributions agree across all 20 filters to within 0.3 mag in the mean and 0.2 mag in width. Residual widths above unity in the intermediate bands (0.92–2.26) honestly flag where the 8-component basis and noise calibration run out — and on the LBG subsample the model shines, with break-filter residuals narrowing to σ = 0.82 (g) and 0.70 (r).

Contribution III

The redshift pipeline — diagnosed, not just scored

The χ² estimator with an empirical coefficient prior achieved σNMAD = 0.1054 with 33.4% catastrophic outliers on 20,000 held-out galaxies — behind the mature codes, and the thesis says so plainly. Its scientific value is the controlled experiment wrapped around it: swapping only the templates for EAZY's within the identical pipeline halved the scatter to 0.0491, isolating the spectral basis as the dominant bottleneck. The prior's proven role: dissolving the z ≈ 8 ceiling artefact where degenerate SEDs pinned galaxies to the top of the grid.

The application

LBGs — the framework on home turf

24,510 g-dropout and 3,867 r-dropout candidates selected via Harikane et al. (2022) cuts, with 61% and 82% purity. The contaminant population wasn't just counted — it was predicted (Balmer-break impostors at z ≈ 0.52 and 0.82, exactly where the D4000 formula places them) and independently convicted by the blue-band test: genuine LBGs are consistent with blank sky blueward of the break (median SNR ≈ 0) while contaminants glow at SNR ≈ 1. On this population, a population-trained prior tripled the redshift estimator's accuracy, and EAZY hit its best numbers anywhere: 0.8% outliers on r-band LBGs.

The fine print — stated, not buried

Five important limitations are flagged explicitly, each with its cause identified and, in most cases, its cure priced.

  • VILA dims at z > 6.5. In the Gunn–Peterson regime, transmission collapses so steeply that the interpolation grid gets left behind. The fix is stated and cheap in principle — a finer redshift grid (Δz = 0.01) and 10,000 sightlines instead of 1,000, as reference works use — it simply needs more compute than an MSc timeline affords.
  • VILA's transmissions are not pure IGM curves. Because the least-squares coefficients absorb part of the absorption first, VILA recovers the residual IGM. Disentangling the two would require fitting coefficients only on bands redward of Lyman-α — and at z ~ 5 COSMOS offers only ~6 such bands, too few to anchor the fit.
  • The NMF basis runs out of vocabulary. Residual widths above unity in several intermediate bands say the 8 components cannot voice every spectral feature those narrow filters hear. A richer template library is the direct remedy.
  • IRAC sits outside the population model. The infrared bands' outsized pull on the fit forced their exclusion; integrating that information without letting it hijack the coefficients remains an open problem.
  • The reference redshifts are themselves photometric. Every σNMAD in the thesis measures agreement with LePhare, not with truth — a caveat that bites hardest exactly where LePhare itself is least reliable (faint, high-z). Spectroscopy is the only escape.

The roadmap

What distinguishes this future-work section is that most items aren't wishes — they are wirings of parts the thesis already built.

Roadmap · from thesis to survey science Near-term wirings, mid-term upgrades, far-term destinations

The highlighted first step is the thesis's own thesis: the population model and the redshift pipeline were built as two halves of one machine. Chapter 7's diagnosis (the prior pulls high-z galaxies toward a low-z mean) is precisely the disease Chapter 6's μ(z) was designed to cure. The wiring diagram already exists — it just wasn't switched on within the MSc timeline.
